# Comprehensive Accounting Rules for Business Investments and Assets

These rules are structured according to GAAP standards, providing clarity for ERP system implementation.

## 1. Investment Valuation
- **Cost Principle**: Investments must be recorded at their historical cost.
  - Example: If a company buys a machine for $50,000, it will be recorded at this amount, regardless of its market value later.

- **Fair Value Measurement**: Investments can be recorded at fair value under certain circumstances.
  - Example: A company holding shares in a publicly traded company may report them at market value.

## 2. Depreciation and Amortization
- **Straight-Line Depreciation**: A method in which the asset’s cost is evenly spread over its useful life.
  - Example: A vehicle costing $30,000 with a useful life of 5 years will have an annual depreciation expense of $6,000.

- **Accelerated Depreciation**: Methods such as Double Declining Balance allow for larger deductions in early years.
  - Example: The same vehicle may depreciate more in the initial years due to accelerated methods.

- **Amortization of Intangible Assets**: Similar to depreciation but applies to intangible assets (e.g., patents).

## 3. Asset Classification
- **Current Assets**: Such as cash and inventory, which are expected to be converted into cash within a year.
- **Non-Current Assets**: Include long-term investments, property, plant, and equipment.

## 4. Impairment of Assets
- **Impairment Testing**: Regular checks to determine if an asset's carrying amount exceeds its recoverable amount.
  - Example: A piece of equipment that can no longer be used effectively may need to be written down.

## 5. Business Investments Documentation
- **Supporting Documents**: Investment transactions should be supported with relevant documentation such as contracts and purchase orders.
- **Regular Reviews**: Periodic reviews of investment performance should occur to ensure proper asset management.

## Conclusion
Implementing these accounting rules adheres to GAAP and assists in optimizing the ERP systems for better financial tracking and reporting.
